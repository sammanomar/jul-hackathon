England

Sennen Cove, North Cornwall

This spot tends to receive a combination of groundswells and windswells, with the most favorable swell direction being from the west. Surfers can expect waves breaking both to the left and right at this beach break.

England

Fistral Beach, North Cornwall

The offshore winds typically blow from the southeast, providing some protection from southwest winds. Fistral Beach benefits from a combination of groundswells and windswells, with the optimal swell direction coming from the west southwest.

Wales

Newgale, Pembrokeshire

is an exposed beach and point break known for its consistently dependable surf, making it suitable for surfing throughout the year. It is most favorable when offshore winds blow from the east northeast.

Southern Ireland

Lahinch, County Clare

It is worth noting that Lahinch offers good surf conditions throughout all stages of the tide. However, it is often crowded due to its popularity. Surfers should be aware of hazards such as rocks and rips when navigating the area.

Northern Ireland

Portrush-West Strand, Antrim

Portrush-West Strand is an exposed beach break known for its reliable surf conditions, although it tends to be mostly flat during the summer. Offshore winds typically come from the southeast, providing some shelter against east winds.

Scotland

Dalmore Bay, Lewis

Dalmore Bay benefits from a combination of groundswells and windswells, with the ideal swell direction originating from the northwest. Surfers can enjoy both left and right reef breaks at this location.

Wales

Llangennith, Gower

Offshore winds typically come from the east, contributing to favorable surfing conditions. Llangennith receives a balanced mix of windswells and groundswells, with the optimal swell angle originating from the west.

Continue Reading

England

Kimmeridge Bay, South Coast

This location does not offer a beach break, but instead provides both left and right-hand reef breaks for surfers to enjoy. The best surfing experience can be found around high tide. It's important to note that Kimmeridge Bay is likely to be crowded when the conditions are favorable, so surfers should be prepared for potential congestion.
